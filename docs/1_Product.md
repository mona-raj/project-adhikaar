# Vision

Everyone has the right to live freely, safely, and with dignity.

Project Adhikaar exists to help vulnerable people safely discover, access, and coordinate trustworthy support while preserving their privacy and agency

# Problem Statement

Many vulnerable people—especially queer and transgender individuals—already have organizations willing to help them.

The problem is finding the right organization at the right time while remaining safe.

Current support networks are often:

- Fragmented across many platforms.
- Difficult to discover during a crisis.
- Inconsistent in quality and responsiveness.
- Difficult to trust for first-time users.
- Not designed around anonymity and user control.

As a result, people delay asking for help or never receive it.

# Users

**Primary Users (MVP)**

People from queer and transgender communities seeking assistance such as:

- Emergency shelter
- Legal assistance
- Mental health support
- Employment support
- Identity documentation
- Community support

**Future Users**

As the platform matures, it may expand to support:

- Women experiencing violence or abuse
- Children requiring protection
- Persons with disabilities
- Elderly individuals
- Migrant workers
- Anyone requiring verified social support services

# Product Principles

The following principles guide every product and engineering decision.

1.  **User Safety First**

    If forced to choose between convenience and user safety, choose safety.

2.  **Privacy by Default**

    Users should reveal only the information necessary to receive assistance.

3.  **User Agency**

    Project Adhikaar facilitates connections. It never removes decision-making from the Help Seeker.

    The Help Seeker decides:
    - whether to submit a request,
    - which organizations may be contacted,
    - what information to share.

4.  **Trust is Earned**

    The platform should earn trust through transparency, reliability, and accurate information—not through marketing.

5.  **Human-Centered**

    Technology exists to support people.

    Whenever human judgment is more appropriate than automation, the product should favor human decision-making.

6.  **Accessibility**

    The platform should remain usable regardless of:
    - device quality,
    - technical literacy,
    - language,
    - financial situation.

# MVP

The first version focuses on solving one problem well.

Users should be able to:

- Learn about available support.
- Submit a Help Request.
- Remain anonymous if they choose.
- Receive suitable organization recommendations.
- Decide which organizations may receive their request.
- Track the progress of their request.
- Access trusted informational resources.

# User Journeys

This section provides a high-level overview of how different users interact with Project Adhikaar.

Detailed interaction flows belong in the [Domain documentation](./2_Domain.md).

## Help Seeker

Typical journey:

1. Discover Project Adhikaar.
2. Learn about available support.
3. Submit a Help Request.
4. Review recommended organizations.
5. Approve one or more referrals.
6. Receive assistance.
7. Track progress.
8. Resolve the request.

## Organization Representative

Typical journey:

1. Receive a referral.
2. Review available case information.
3. Accept or decline.
4. Coordinate assistance.
5. Update progress where appropriate.
6. Mark assistance complete.
